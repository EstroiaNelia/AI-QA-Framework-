## Decisions

- The Purpose and Scope sections were drafted.
- The Framework Principles section was drafted.
- The AI System Quality Dimensions section was drafted.
- The AI QA Lifecycle section was drafted.
- The framework will use `AI-based system` as the preferred term for the overall system.
- The term `AI component` will be used when referring to a specific AI-related part of the system.
- The lifecycle section was adjusted to support both traditional Machine Learning and Generative AI contexts.
- Section 6 was drafted to map QA activities to each AI QA Lifecycle stage.
- The activities are intended to be adaptable to traditional Machine Learning, Generative AI, third-party AI components and AI-enabled software products.
- Section 7 was drafted to define how evidence and artefacts support AI QA decisions.
- Evidence is treated as central to making quality decisions observable, reviewable and traceable.
- A minimum evidence set was introduced to avoid making the framework too bureaucratic.

## Review Notes

- The document is coherent from the initial definition through the AI QA Lifecycle section.
- The sequence Definition, Purpose, Scope, Principles, Quality Dimensions and Lifecycle is logical.
- The wording remains practical, vendor-independent and QA-oriented.
- The distinction between model-level concerns and system-level concerns is clear.
- Security, privacy and misuse risk are addressed from a QA perspective and may require collaboration with specialist teams.

- The artefact categories are practical and reusable across different AI contexts.
- The minimum evidence set helps distinguish essential evidence from optional artefacts.
- The section should later be connected with reusable templates.
